Bio:

Ayesha Munot

India Mumbai

Ayesha Munot is a sophomore of the Graphic Design major in California College of the Art.
Ayesha fell love in design and art before she comes to America. She chooses to study abroad for
further study of her interest. She wasn’t sure to be a graphic design student until she
actually is a student in CCA. She builds her love on graphic design by the time she took the
class of graphic design. Aysha wants to be an editor for a famous magazine company, in the future.
She also wants to travel around the world on a day, and learn different knowledge of graphic design
by traveling. To find her own design.

Conversation:

What is ‘good’ screen-based design?

The website that is Original, have more interactive with the user. The Website that is colorful,
and 3dimention

How is art-making online different then art-making offline?

The physical interaction is better when I work online, the feeling of using different materials,
and each material has its unique texture. To work online is more just a visual interaction, but work
online will be much faster.

what do you think is the ‘purpose(s)’ of the web?

To improve communication between people in the different area. Web also will help share the good
ideals around the world and can help reach the international audience for artist or designers.

How has your view of the internet / How you experience being online changed over time?

I feel like the future of the internet is unpredictable, the change of the internet world is so fast.
You will never know what can happen tomorrow, but the growth of the internet is also a growth of the danger.
There is no privacy on the internet. For the good part of the, grow of the internet is the communication
between people become easier.

How does the web impact graphic design?

The impact is revolutionized how people view graphic design. A lot of the great ideas can be converted to
a real product in a quick and cheap way. Web makes everything more accessible and provides a wider audience.
Web also makes the graphic design more general to the public.

Do graphic designer have to learn coding?

To learn what is coding and how to coding is helpful, but not all of the graphic designers have to know coding.
I personally think web design is interesting,and I willing to learn more of the coding to make better designs.

What is your favorite Adobe program?

Photoshop, because I like working with images, and I want to work with the magazines, so image making is important.
I like image making of the Typography too.
